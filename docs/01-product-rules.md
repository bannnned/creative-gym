# Product Rules

## Principles

1. Build a creative training app, not a social feed.
2. Keep the MVP focused on photography.
3. Prefer weekly rhythm and habit formation over viral mechanics.
4. Keep rooms small enough to feel personal.
5. Avoid public popularity signals in the MVP.
6. Use Creative Gym language consistently.
7. Make each workflow simple enough to understand without explanation screens.

## Required Product Terms

Use these terms in user-facing copy:

- Creative Gym
- Weekly Workout
- Gym Room
- Creative Streak
- Coach Notes
- Creative Form

Use these meanings:

- Challenge / Weekly Workout: the public creative task.
- Gym Room: a small group of participants assigned to the same challenge.
- Submission: exactly one uploaded creative work for a room.
- Voting: structured evaluation after the submission phase.
- Results: the final room outcome after voting ends.

## MVP Product Scope

The MVP must include:

- active weekly photo workouts;
- challenge details;
- joining a gym room;
- room details;
- placeholders for photo upload;
- backend and database structure ready for upload, voting, and results.

The MVP must not include:

- comments;
- subscriptions;
- payments;
- public likes feed;
- complex ranking;
- push notifications;
- music challenges;
- video challenges;
- admin panel;
- AI features;
- social follows;
- chat.

## UX Direction

The interface should be:

- clean;
- modern;
- mobile-first;
- calm;
- slightly inspired by gym training;
- focused on weekly progress.

Avoid building an endless feed.

Avoid making the product feel like competitive social media.
